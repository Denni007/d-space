Enabling Server-Side Tracking For Your Business

Start preparing from now your business for the new coming up cookieless era and get around new modern web browser restrictions. We help you run a seamless server side tracking setup with Google Tag Manager.

What is server-side tagging?

Server-Side Tagging is a new way to use Google Tag Manager that allows you to move measurement tags from your website or app to a server-side processing container

However, with the rising awareness of user privacy and browser clients offering more functionalities to safeguard it, digital marketers realizing the implications of it on their data, and insights. The efforts to prevent 3rd party data from being misused have forced Google to offer a server-side solution for their Google Tag Manager product.

Google Tag Manager Server Side will solve many issues, and at the same time give you unprecedented control over the data. With Google Tag Manager Server Side, the data created by a user is no longer sent directly to various marketing vendors such as Facebook, Adform, or even Google. Every data element collected on your company website will be sent to a server-side endpoint that you control.

The advantages of Server-Side

Adaptation to the end of third-party cookies.
Improved page loading speed.
Data collection maintained on Safari and AdBlockers.
Increased security of consumer data.
Full control over data sharing.
Optimized management of multi-touch attribution.
Data driven for better performance.
Avoid the capture of prohibited information by third parties.

Description of Server-side Configuration

The end of third-party cookies and the growing use of AdBlockers make it difficult to track users on your site, limiting your ability to optimize multi-touch attribution and drive data.

Not adapting your tracking strategy can lead to a loss of visibility and a decrease in the performance of your site. The server-side is the solution to circumvent these obstacles.

Here are the key stages of the project:

  1. Assessment of your current configuration and identification of needs
  2. Setting up the server-side infrastructure with Google Tag Manager
  3. Migration of existing tags and pixels to server-side
  4. Optimized page load speed
  5. Configuring cookies and tracking for Safari and AdBlockers
  6. Tests and verifications to ensure the reliability of the data collected
  7. Training and support for optimal use of GTM server-side
