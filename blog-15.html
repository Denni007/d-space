What Are GA4 Annotations?

April 2, 2025
By
  Hevin Dhokariya

What Are GA4 Annotations?

Google has recently introduced a valuable feature in Google Analytics 4 (GA4)â€”Annotations. These annotations are brief notes added directly to your analytics reports, making it simpler to document and understand changes in your data.

Why Use Annotations?

Easier Data Interpretation: Quickly identify why traffic fluctuates, conversions dip, or campaigns succeed by providing direct context within your data.

Enhanced Team Collaboration: Share important insights across your team easily and effectively, ensuring everyone is informed about key events and changes.

How to Add an Annotation in GA4

  1. Navigate to Reports: In GA4, open the "Reports" tab.
  2. Add Annotation: Click the annotation icon (sticky note icon) at the top-right corner of your report.
  3. Enter Annotation Details:
    • Title: Up to 60 characters.
    • Description: Provide context or details (up to 150 characters).
    • Date or Range: Select a specific date or date range relevant to your note.
    • Color: Choose a color for easy categorization.
  4. Save Annotation: Click "Create annotation" to save.

Managing Annotations

All annotations are visible in reports containing line graphs. You can hover over the annotation icon for quick details or click it for more comprehensive information in the "Annotations Viewer" panel. For managing all annotations, go to Admin > Annotations, where you can create, edit, delete, or export them as needed.

Automatic System Annotations

Google Analytics may automatically add annotations to inform you about significant events affecting data. These system-generated annotations can't be edited or deleted, ensuring you are always aware of major issues or changes.

Best Practices for Using Annotations

  • Maintain clear and consistent titles for quick identification.
  • Regularly update annotations to reflect the most accurate data context.
  • Encourage your team to utilize annotations actively.
